# Home Page Override — Qualified Inquiry Landing

## Conversion narrative

1. **Hero / fit:** state the commercial value, identify Ahsan, show one person with two outputs, and present one primary inquiry CTA.
2. **Credibility rail:** surface factual proof immediately—education, three real roles, and recognized competitive/certification signals.
3. **Selected engineering cases:** explain the problem, what Ahsan built, his contribution, stack, and source where available.
4. **AI video practice:** show clear deliverables and who each is useful for; present it as the supporting practice, not a separate persona.
5. **Engagement paths:** let a buyer self-select an AI build or AI video brief with explicit starting inputs.
6. **Process and About proof:** reduce uncertainty about working together and link to the full factual story.
7. **Contact close:** repeat one direct inquiry action with email fallback.

## Signature element

**The operator frame:** Ahsan's portrait sits inside a precise black studio frame. Two thin output rails leave the same portrait—cyan for engineered systems and yellow for produced video—making “one person, two capabilities” immediately understandable without two personas.

## Hero copy direction

- Eyebrow: `AI engineer + AI video producer`
- Thesis: `AI systems built to ship. Video built to make the idea land.`
- Support: direct, factual, buyer-oriented; mention products, agents, automation, explainers, demos, and ads without claiming results not provided.
- Primary CTA: `Discuss an AI build`
- Secondary CTA: `See selected work`
- Supporting microcopy: `Have a video brief instead? Start there →`

## Visual rules

- Hero is primarily paper/ink, not a four-color poster.
- Red appears on the primary CTA and one high-intent closing field.
- Yellow appears in the video output rail and video capability cues.
- Cyan appears in system diagrams, source-link focus, and technical metadata.
- Project proof lives on a single ink stage with paper panels and thin signal rails.
- Use the approved portrait in the hero; reuse it later only as a small About proof crop, not another dominant image.

## Conversion rules

- A visitor should understand who, what, proof, and next action within one viewport.
- Each project must state Ahsan's contribution, not only the technology.
- Links say what they open (`View DocuSync source`) instead of `Learn more`.
- Service descriptions name a buyer use case, not just a production technique.
- The email action remains available in navigation, the engagement section, and the final close without presenting multiple equal primary CTAs at once.
